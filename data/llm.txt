Large Language Models (LLMs)

Definition
LLM stands for Large Language Model. It is an artificial intelligence system trained on very large amounts of text to understand and generate human-like language.

How It Works
LLMs learn patterns, grammar, context, and relationships between words from massive datasets. They do not think like humans, but they generate responses by predicting the most likely next words based on what they have learned.

Common Uses
LLMs are used for:
- answering questions
- writing and editing text
- summarizing information
- translating languages
- generating code
- brainstorming ideas

Importance
Large Language Models are important because they make it easier to interact with technology using natural language. They are widely used in chatbots, education, search tools, customer support, and software development.

Conclusion
In summary, LLMs are powerful AI systems that can perform many language-related tasks efficiently. Their growing use is transforming how people learn, work, and communicate.
